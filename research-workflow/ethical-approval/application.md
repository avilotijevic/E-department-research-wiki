## Workflow for Ethical Approval

This is a step-by-step workflow for applying for ethical approval:

1. Add a new study in the research portal.
2. Click on **Ethical Review** and then **Open EC Request**.  
   This will take you to the EC Request portal.
3. Click on **Start new request** and select the study you just created in the research portal.
4. Next to your study, click the green **Fill out** button.
5. Complete the questionnaire and submit the request for approval by your **Principal Investigator (PI)**.

---

## Fast-track procedure

The second question in the application asks whether your study is eligible for the **fast-track procedure**.

This procedure assesses the potential ethical risks involved in your study. If the study is classified as **low risk**, it is exempt from full ethical review, which significantly speeds up the approval process.

Many standard experiments conducted at Experimental Psychology meet the fast-track criteria, **except** for:

- EEG studies
- Eye-tracking studies

For studies with any risk of **incidental findings**, the ECP may require a full review in order to assess how you plan to deal with these risks.

## Uploading documents

You will need to upload several documents. Templates can be downloaded directly from the questionnaire.  
These documents are required **regardless of whether you apply for fast-track or not**.

Required documents:

- **Information sheet**  
  Information provided to the participant about the study.
- **Informed consent form**  
  Consent to be completed by the participant.
- **Research plan**  
  Description of the research to be conducted.
- **Data management plan**  
  Plan for collecting, storing, and publishing data.

## Important considerations

- Much of the information requested relates to the university’s obligation to comply with **GDPR**.
- It is strongly recommended to familiarize yourself with the legal definitions of:
  - anonymous data
  - pseudonymous data
  - personal data
- **SONA studies are never fully anonymous**, because participant remuneration requires processing a SONA ID, which counts as personal data.
- Paper informed consent forms are discouraged:
  - they rely on identifiable personal data (name/signature)
  - it is often unclear how long they must be stored
- Both online and lab studies can use **digital consent procedures**, where the experiment only starts after consent is given.
- GDPR requires **separate consent for processing personal data**, in addition to consent for participation.  
  It is considered best practice to ask for this in a separate question.
