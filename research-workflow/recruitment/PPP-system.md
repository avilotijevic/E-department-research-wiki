# Paid Participants Pool (PPP) system

Here you can find the relevant resources on how to apply for a PPP account and how to implement
your study in the Paid Participants Pool (PPP).

The **Psychology Paid Participant Pool (PPP)** consists of participants external to the first-year
SONA system, such as students from other faculties and non-student populations
(e.g. working adults or older participants). Participants are typically financially compensated
for their participation.

The default language of the PPP is **Dutch**. For English-language studies, a language
restriction can be set.

If you have questions that are not answered here, contact the PPP administration
(**Norbert Börger**) via:  
**proefpersonen-psy@rug.nl**

## Workflow for the PPP system

Before running a study via the PPP, **ethical approval is required**, and the study must be
implemented in the **PPP-specific SONA system**.  
This system is different from the first-year SONA system.

Before implementing your study in the PPP, you will need the **Ethics Approval code**
(e.g. `PSY-1213-S-4567`).

This is the step-by-step workflow for the PPP system:

1. Fill out the **SONA request survey** with the details of your study  
   (description of the experiment, duration, researcher information, etc.).

   This step is required for both SONA and PPP studies. In this survey you:
   - register your study
   - request PPP researcher accounts

   There are two types of accounts:
   - **PI account** – a limited account for formal responsibility and checking the study
   - **Researcher account** – required for creating, managing, and running studies

   *Note: approval may take one or two days.*

2. Once the study is reviewed, you will receive a **Login Information email**
   containing your username and password.

3. Log in to the **PPP system** using the credentials from the login email.

4. Add your study by clicking **Add new study** and filling out the study details.

5. Once the study is successfully implemented and visible under the **My studies** tab,
   you can view its status.

6. Initially, the study status will be:
   - *Not approved*
   - *Timeslots not available*

   This means the study is **not yet visible to participants**. To change this:

   1. Click **Timeslots**  
      (`My studies → View → Timeslots`) and add at least one timeslot.  
      The status should now show: *Not approved*, *Timeslots available*.

   2. Click on your study name  
      (`My studies → Study information`) and click **Send request** under *Study status*.  
      *Note: this approval may take one or two days.*

## Payment of participants

Participants recruited via the PPP must be paid using the university’s **official payment procedure**.

Important points:

- Researchers must collect **minimal personal information** for payment purposes:
  - e-mail address (required)
  - student number (optional, if applicable)
- Payments are handled via the **FedRbp system (https://rbpreg.gai.rug.nl/)**:  
  https://rbpreg.gai.rug.nl/
- When using Qualtrics, payment information should be collected in a **separate survey**,
  typically via a redirect at the end of the study.

---

## Indicative compensation rates

### Online studies
- 15 minutes: €2
- 30 minutes: €4
- 60 minutes: €7
- 90 minutes: €10

### Lab studies
- 30 minutes: €5
- 60 minutes: €8
- 90 minutes: €11

For longer studies, compensation should scale accordingly.  
Always advertise the **maximum duration** of the study.
