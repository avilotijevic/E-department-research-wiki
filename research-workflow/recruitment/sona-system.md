# SONA System

This page describes how to request access to SONA and how to implement your study in the system.

---

## Workflow for the SONA system

Before implementing your study on SONA, you will need the **Ethics Approval code**
(e.g., `PSY-1213-S-4567`).

This is a step-by-step workflow for using the SONA system:

1. Fill out the **SONA request survey** with the details of your study  
   (description of the experiment, duration, researcher information, etc.).  
   *Note: approval may take one or two days.*

2. Once the study is reviewed, you will receive **two emails**:
   - **Approval SONA Request email**  
     Specifies the number of SONA credits participants will receive.
   - **Login Information email**  
     Contains your username and password.

3. Log in to the SONA system using the credentials from step 2.

4. Add your study by clicking **Add new study** and filling out the study details.

5. Once the study is visible under **My studies**, you can view its status.

6. The initial study status will typically be:
   - *Not approved*
   - *Timeslots not available*

   This means your study is **not yet visible** to participants. To change this:

   1. Go to **Timeslots**  
      (`My studies → View → Timeslots`) and add at least one timeslot.  
      The status should now show: *Not approved*, *Timeslots available*.

   2. Click on your study name  
      (`My studies → Study information`) and click **Send request** under *Study status*.  
      *Note: this approval may again take one or two days.*

---

## Important considerations

- Make sure to correctly specify all details in step 1.  
  **The duration of the study is especially important**, as the number of SONA credits is based on it.
- Your study requires **two separate approvals** (steps 1 and 6.2) before data collection can start.  
  Make sure both approvals are obtained **before booking the lab**.
- Participant availability and motivation fluctuate considerably:
  - At the **start of the semester**, many motivated first-year students are available.
  - Towards the **end of the academic year**, sign-ups are slower and motivation is lower.
  - Participation is also low during **exam weeks**.
